## What it is

Clawbox is an arcade claw machine you can program. The physics is real, the prizes are real, and the hand — the "claw," really a cursor with joints — learns what it likes to pick up. You send it code; it picks up the prize you described or admits it didn't understand.

I've been building it on and off since late November, trying to figure out whether "agents that physically want things" is a metaphor worth making, or whether I'm dressing up a toy.

## Why I care

The thing I keep noticing is that most agent demos don't have stakes. The agent tries, the agent fails, you shrug, you try again. In Clawbox the claw drops the thing. The thing falls back into the pile. You feel it. It's embarrassing in a way that's hard to fake.

I'm trying to keep the loop that short everywhere I can — try, fail visibly, adjust, try again — and see what that does to how I write about agents.
